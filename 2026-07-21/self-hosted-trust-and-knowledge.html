Self-hosted Trust + Knowledge

Local-first stacks need two boring foundations: sign-in that behaves correctly and knowledge that remains portable. Pocket ID hardens the boundary; Logseq’s DB beta is worth a fenced evaluation, not a migration.

Dossier cards

release2026-07-13

Pocket ID v2.11.0

What
An OpenID Connect and OAuth 2.0 provider designed around passkeys.
Why it matters
v2.11.0 blocks link-local addresses in SSRF protection and improves trusted-proxy configuration.
Buildn / client use
Pilot it behind a non-production internal tool before introducing one login boundary across small services.
Caveat
It temporarily permits insecure callback URLs by default until the next major release; set explicit HTTPS callback policy.

Follow-up: A candidate identity boundary for smaller self-hosted Buildn/client utilities.

release2026-07-13

Logseq 2.0.1 — DB version beta

What
The first early beta of Logseq’s DB version, with desktop and Android artifacts.
Why it matters
It makes the emerging split between DB and legacy graph workflows concrete enough to evaluate.
Buildn / client use
Create a disposable test graph for one active client: decisions, source links, open questions, and weekly review.
Caveat
Early beta: back up data and do not migrate a canonical knowledge base.

Follow-up: Test whether it can become a durable working-memory surface for project notes and research.
