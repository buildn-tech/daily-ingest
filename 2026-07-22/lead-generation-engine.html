Lead Generation Engine: from scattered sources to approved revenue opportunities

A compliant, evidence-led system for discovering businesses, cleaning and enriching their records, finding timing signals, scoring fit, and routing only verified opportunities into outreach.

The decision

Build a golden-record and qualification engine, not a scraping machine

Lead generation compounds when every candidate becomes a traceable account record: what we know, where it came from, when it was checked, why it fits, and whether it is safe to contact. Collection adapters are replaceable. The durable asset is the clean, permission-aware record plus the feedback loop from real revenue outcomes.

Discover broadly

Registries, licensed datasets, company sites, associations, public signals and permitted APIs.

Qualify rigorously

Normalize, dedupe, enrich, verify, score and require evidence before outreach.

Learn continuously

Use replies, meetings, wins, losses and opt-outs to improve ICP and source allocation.

North star: one verified, relevant account with a defensible reason to engage is more valuable than dozens of untraceable contacts.

System shape

The full loop

Design rule: each arrow is a durable run with inputs, outputs, source provenance, cost, timestamps and an operator. No silent mutation of a lead record.

The moat

Golden record, provenance and deduplication

Canonical account fields

  • Canonical company name
  • Root domain and verified website
  • Country, region, legal entity ID
  • Industry and ICP taxonomy
  • Size / revenue band with confidence
  • Locations and operating status
  • Owner, lifecycle stage and score

Every claim carries lineage

  • Field name and observed value
  • Source URL/provider and source type
  • Collected and last-verified timestamp
  • Confidence and extraction method
  • Usage / license notes
  • Evidence excerpt or snapshot pointer
  • Retention and deletion policy

Deduplication hierarchy: exact root domain + registry ID first; then domain; then normalized name + address/phone; finally a review queue for fuzzy matches. Never discard the source records. Link them to the canonical entity.

AI is a multiplier, not a source

Use models to structure evidence and prioritize work

High-value AI work

  • Extract services and categories from first-party sites
  • Classify ICP fit against a controlled taxonomy
  • Summarize a target account in CRM-ready language
  • Detect supported triggers and contradictions
  • Recommend likely buyer roles, marked as hypotheses
  • Draft a specific personalization angle from cited evidence
  • Route low-confidence records to review

AI must not decide

  • Invent an email, owner, revenue or event
  • Claim an unsupported buying signal
  • Override a suppression / opt-out
  • Make the legal-basis determination alone
  • Autonomously launch high-volume outreach
  • Turn source snippets into reusable data without usage checks
  • Convert “unknown” into “valid”
Prompt contract: every AI output should contain claim, evidence_url, evidence_excerpt, confidence, unknowns, and recommended_next_action. If evidence is absent, the model must output unknown.

Operational recipe

The enrichment and qualification waterfall

1
Define the ICP and exclusions.
Specify industry, size, geography, business model, buyer role, offer, buying triggers and explicit skips. This is a versioned policy, not a loose prompt.
2
Discover accounts from 2–3 appropriate sources.
Create candidates with source provenance. Over-source deliberately, then qualify aggressively.
3
Normalize and resolve duplicates.
Create or update the golden account, preserve raw source records, and queue ambiguous matches.
4
Enrich company facts first.
Industry, service lines, locations, stack, public signal, legal status and account context from permitted sources.
5
Find the role, then enrich the contact.
Use a conditional multi-provider waterfall. Do not pay every provider for every record. Prefer business and role contacts where appropriate.
6
Verify contactability immediately before outreach.
Only verified email can enter an approved queue. Invalid, disposable and uncertain results are held back. Treat catch-all conservatively.
7
Score with evidence and a why-now.
Fit alone is not “hot.” Require a source-backed trigger, reachable appropriate role and acceptable verification state.
8
Run the human-safe gate.
Dedupe against CRM, customers, open opportunities and permanent suppression lists; apply geography and channel rules; approve, suppress or recycle.
9
Route, measure and recalibrate.
Send approved records to the CRM/outreach queue with context. Feed back response, meeting, qualified opportunity, win/loss and opt-out outcomes.

Scoring

Make “Hot” a strict operational label

DimensionQuestionEvidence requirement
FitDoes the account meet the ICP’s industry, size, geography, model and exclusions?Company website, registry or licensed provider; evidence retained.
TimingIs there a credible reason to engage now?Specific recent launch, hiring, expansion, vendor gap or stated need with source URL.
RoleIs there an appropriate decision-maker or buying-influencer role?Role validated by provider or first-party/public evidence; label uncertainty.
ContactabilityCan the selected channel be used safely?Recent validation state, channel policy and no suppression match.
Offer relevanceCan the offer solve a visible problem or opportunity?One concise evidence-backed relevance statement.
HOT

Strong fit + verified contact + supported trigger + relevant offer. Ready for approval.

WARM

Fit and viable contact, but a softer, older or incomplete trigger. Nurture or research further.

COLD / SKIP

Loose fit, missing contact, no signal, duplicate, closed business, policy failure or explicit disqualifier.

Non-negotiable controls

Compliance and reputation are product features

Data governance

  • Record source URL/provider and date for every material field
  • Retain use restrictions and purpose alongside the data
  • Minimize personal data and set retention windows
  • Keep raw-source and canonical-record history
  • Make suppression global, durable and checked before every send
  • Review source terms whenever an adapter changes

Outbound controls

  • Accurate sender identity and headers
  • Clear, functioning opt-out in each commercial email
  • Valid physical postal address where required
  • Prompt, permanent opt-out processing
  • Domain warm-up, bounce monitoring and conservative send limits
  • No automatic sending from weak or uncited AI conclusions

The FTC’s CAN-SPAM guidance explicitly covers commercial B2B email and requires accurate headers, a physical address, a clear opt-out and prompt honoring of opt-outs. Jurisdiction-specific rules, including GDPR/PECR considerations in the UK/EU, must be assessed for the actual geography, channel and data processed.

Recommended next move

Run a narrow 30-day pilot and measure the whole loop

Pick one ICP

One geography, one segment, one offer and one named buying trigger. Example: a specific vertical of multi-location operators needing workflow automation.

Set a quality target

Start with 25 verified, evidence-backed accounts, not 2,500 names. Track source-to-approved-lead yield and cost.

Instrument feedback

Capture reply, positive reply, meeting, SQL, opportunity, win/loss, bounce and opt-out per source and segment.

Decide with data

After 30 days, keep only sources and scoring rules that produce qualified conversations, not just populated fields.

Pilot acceptance criteria

  • Every approved lead has a canonical account, source lineage and last-verified date.
  • Every “Hot” lead has clear ICP evidence, a source-backed why-now and verified contactability.
  • Zero suppressed or duplicate CRM contacts enter outreach.
  • Every outreach result is written back to the record within a defined SLA.
  • Weekly dashboard shows yield, cost, data-quality failures and revenue outcomes by source.
First scoping input required: define the initial ICP in one sentence: who we sell to, what offer we lead with, which geography, and what event makes them timely.
