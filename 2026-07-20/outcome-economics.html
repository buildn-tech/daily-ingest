A scorecard for the AI age

OPENAI · 2026-07-17 · BLOG

What it is

A four-part frame: work completed, full cost per successful task, dependability, and value as usage scales.

Why it matters

A cheap call that produces rework is not cheap. The unit of comparison is an accepted outcome.

Buildn / client use

Instrument one recurring delivery flow: report, PR, research brief, or QA fix.

Follow-up

Define “accepted” before comparing model routes for two weeks.

Caveat: The framework is vendor guidance; its model-performance claims need independent, task-specific verification.

Governance

How to manage AI investments in the agentic era

OPENAI · 2026-07-14 · BLOG

What it is

A rollout sequence for usage visibility, outcome ROI, controls, phased investment, and capacity planning.

Why now

Scheduled agents cross tools and take actions; a chat-era “usage” dashboard is insufficient.

Buildn / client use

Give each agent an approved context set, tool allowlist, action boundary, escalation owner, and spend ceiling.

Follow-up

Write one action matrix before expanding a client workflow beyond draft mode.

Caveat: Central administration helps but does not replace application-level audit trails and human approval for irreversible actions.
