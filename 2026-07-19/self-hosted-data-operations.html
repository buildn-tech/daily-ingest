breaking change2026-06-18 / rolls July 6–10

Self-hosted API_EXTERNAL_URL now includes /auth/v1

What: Self-hosted defaults move API_EXTERNAL_URL, OAuth callback construction, and SAML routes onto the /auth/v1 convention.

Why it matters: Customized compose files or SAML IdP metadata can break even when the final ordinary OAuth callback appears unchanged.

Buildn / client use

Prevent a client login outage during a self-hosted image refresh.

Follow-up

Diff .env and docker-compose against the new defaults; verify SAML ACS and metadata URLs in staging.

Caveat: Platform-hosted projects are unaffected; the risk is concentrated in customized self-hosted deployments.

migration2026-07-01

Postgres 14 support ends; projects move to Postgres 17

What: Deprecated projects are automatically upgraded; unsupported-extension cases can instead be paused and stop serving traffic.

Why it matters: A version upgrade is not just maintenance when extensions, downtime, and application compatibility are involved.

Buildn / client use

Turn database version inventory into a client deliverable with a tested restore and rollback plan.

Follow-up

Run pg_extension on every legacy database, then rehearse the upgrade against a Postgres 17 project.

Caveat: Small databases may still need downtime; larger projects can require an hour or more.

configuration2026-07-09

Connection logging defaults to off

What: log_connections changes from on to off for new projects and existing Free/Pro projects, controllable via dashboard and Management API.

Why it matters: Reduced log noise is good, but an incident playbook that depends on connection events needs an explicit replacement or override.

Buildn / client use

Make logging policy intentional per production client rather than inheriting tier-dependent defaults.

Follow-up

Decide whether connection/disconnection events belong in your audit or incident workflow, then set config through the API.

Caveat: Existing Teams and Enterprise configurations are unchanged; do not assume all projects behave alike.
